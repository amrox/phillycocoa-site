Cocoa Foundations Workshop Wrap-Up

May 20th, 2009.

The inaugural and slightly experimental Cocoa Foundations Workshop was a success! The workshop sold out with limited advertising and promotion, and received generally positive feedback from the 15 attendees.

Here is what was covered:

Session 1 Definitions Xcode Basic Setup First Program C Review Object-Oriented Programming Messaging Memory Management Basics Accessors & Object Initialization Foundation Homework: TwitterSearch

Session 2 More Pointers Memory Management Protocols Categories Declared Properties Foundation Intro to AppKit Grand Finale: TwitterSearchTouch

Special thanks Mike Zornek, Aaron Evans, Dave Martorana, and Jason Allum for their help planning and executing the workshop, as well as Alex Hillman and IndyHall for their support.

Of course, there always of room for improvement. If you have any questions or feedback about the workshop, please contact Andy Mroczkowski or any of the other organizers with your thoughts.

Stay tuned for next installment of Cocoa Foundations Workshop or other events covering more advanced topics.

And finally, one last word of appreciation to all the attendees. Thanks for being a great group!

CocoaHeads May 2009 Meeting Recap

May 19th, 2009.

There were some great presentations this month. If you didn't make it, you missed out!

WWDC09

A few items regarding WWDC:

The IndyHall Labs team is going to WWDC09!

Events and Parties SFMacIndie, Sunday June 7, 5-9pm iPhone Intelligence Party Monday June 8, 7-11pm * WebKit Party, Wednesday June 10 7-10pm

Watch the mailing list or follow us on twitter for further updates.

Show and Tell

First, Dave Martorana (@themartorana]) discussed how WebKit is used in Multiplex to rapidly create compelling interface elements.

Next, Mike Zornek (@zorn) showed his use of WebKit and the Raphaël JavaScript library for generating high-quality vector-based charts in his upcoming application, ProfitTrain.

Finally, Andy Mroczkowski (@amrox) demoed a technique that helps developers run a subset of unit tests in Xcode, as well as some testing basics.
